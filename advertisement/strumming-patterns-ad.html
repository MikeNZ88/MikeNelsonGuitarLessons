Guitar Strumming Patterns

The Golden Rule of Strumming

Your strumming hand should move continuously down-up-down-up like a metronome.
The magic: not every movement needs to hit the strings!

Consistent hand movement = Perfect timing

Note: We're not really "skipping" - your hand keeps moving, it just doesn't hit the strings on ghost strums.

Essential Strumming Tips

Use your wrist, not your whole arm for strumming!
⬇️ Downstrokes
• Hit all strings (unless avoiding specific ones)
• Strike firmly and consistently
• Create strong, accented beats
• Full, rich sound from all strings
⬆️ Upstrokes
• Often hit 3-4 higher strings only
• Lighter touch than downstrokes
• Natural to miss some strings (the thicker ones)
• Provide lighter "and" beats
Pro Tip: This natural difference between downstrokes (fuller, all strings) and upstrokes (lighter, fewer strings) automatically creates musical accent and rhythm!
